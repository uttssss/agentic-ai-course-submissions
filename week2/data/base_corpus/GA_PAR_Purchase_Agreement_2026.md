# Georgia Association of Realtors — Purchase and Sale Agreement Guidelines (2026)

## Binding Agreement Date
The Binding Agreement Date is the date on which a party communicates notice of final acceptance of all terms of the agreement to the other party. All contractual time periods are calculated from the Binding Agreement Date unless a specific section states otherwise. Day one is the day after the Binding Agreement Date. If a deadline falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or federal holiday, it extends to the next business day.

## Due Diligence Period
The Due Diligence Period gives the buyer an absolute right to terminate the agreement for any reason or no reason, provided written notice of termination is delivered before the period expires. The Due Diligence Period begins on the day after the Binding Agreement Date and runs for the number of calendar days stated in the special stipulations. During this period the buyer may conduct inspections, review HOA documents, and assess the property. If the buyer terminates within the Due Diligence Period, the earnest money is refunded to the buyer.

## Inspection and Repair Requests
Requests for repairs based on the home inspection must be submitted in writing to the seller before the Due Diligence Period expires. After the Due Diligence Period ends, the buyer generally loses the right to demand repairs or to terminate based on the property's condition. The seller is not obligated to agree to any repair request; unresolved repair items may be negotiated by amendment before the period closes.

## Financing Contingency
When a financing contingency is included, the buyer's obligation to close is conditioned on obtaining a loan approval on the terms stated. The buyer must apply for financing within five (5) days of the Binding Agreement Date and pursue approval in good faith. If the lender's final loan approval is not issued by the financing deadline stated in the agreement, the buyer may terminate and recover earnest money. Waiving the financing contingency removes this protection.

## Appraisal Contingency
If an appraisal contingency is included and the property appraises below the purchase price, the buyer may (a) terminate the agreement and recover earnest money, (b) proceed at the contract price by covering the gap, or (c) negotiate a price reduction. Without an appraisal contingency, a low appraisal does not by itself entitle the buyer to terminate with earnest money refunded.

## Closing and Possession
The closing date is the date specified in the agreement on which the deed is delivered and funds disburse. Possession transfers at closing unless a separate occupancy agreement applies. Failure of the seller to complete agreed-upon repairs before closing entitles the buyer to remedies including delaying closing, an escrow holdback for the cost of repairs, or pursuing the seller for breach.

## Earnest Money
Earnest money is held in escrow and applied to the purchase at closing. It is refundable to the buyer when the buyer terminates within the Due Diligence Period or under a valid contingency. It may be forfeited if the buyer defaults outside of any contingency.
