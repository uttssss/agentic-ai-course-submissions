# Maplewood Community Association — HOA Bylaws (Fulton County, GA)

## Application and Approval
Prospective owners must submit a community association application. The association has up to fifteen (15) days from receipt of a complete application to approve or deny. The association may not unreasonably withhold approval. Membership in the association is mandatory for all owners.

## Application Denial While Under Contract
If the association denies an application after the buyer is already under contract, the buyer's remedies depend on whether the purchase agreement includes an HOA-approval contingency. With such a contingency, the buyer may terminate and recover earnest money if denial occurs before the contingency deadline, which is generally tied to the Due Diligence Period. Without the contingency, the buyer must rely on the general Due Diligence termination right and may lose remedies once that period expires.

## Document Review
The association provides governing documents, current budget, and assessment schedule upon request. Buyers are expected to review these during the Due Diligence Period. Outstanding assessments transfer with the property unless resolved at closing.

## Architectural and Use Restrictions
Owners must obtain Architectural Review Committee approval before exterior modifications. Use restrictions, including leasing caps and pet limits, are enforceable and run with the land.
